Bitcoin is the first cryptocurrency ever created that became used globally and is mainly used as a store of value among digital asset investors. It is also called digital gold and has a price of thousands of dollars per token. Bitcoin is the most valuable and often accepted cryptocurrency. It uses a Proof-of-Work consensus mechanism, meaning that Bitcoin miners compete against each other to verify transactions and the first to do so receives BTC as a reward.

 

Ethereum is a decentralized, open-source blockchain with smart contract functionality. Ether (ETH) is the native token that facilitates operations on the network, and developers can deploy their own digital assets on its ERC-20 standard. Contrary to Bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH) uses the Proof-of-Stake consensus mechanism, which isnt energy-intensive and allows owners of the tokens to be transaction validators. The more ETH tokens a validator has, the more likely it will be chosen to verify transactions and, thus, to receive rewards.

Bitcoin (BTC) Vs. Ethereum (ETH) - What is the Difference?

Although Ethereum and Bitcoin are both cryptocurrencies and similar in many ways, Bitcoin was designed as a currency and a store of value, while Ethereum is intended to deploy complex smart contracts and decentralized applications. Ethereum transactions may contain executable code, while data affixed to Bitcoin transactions is only used to record transaction information. The block times between the cryptocurrencies also differ as well as their consensus mechanisms as Bitcoin uses Proof-of-Works whilst Ethereum uses Proof-of-Stake.
